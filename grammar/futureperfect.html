Future Continuous Tense

1. The Future Continuous Tense

The future continuous (also known as the future progressive) tense is often used in English as a way to talk about an action or event happening at a given point in the future It indicates that something will occur in the future and continue for an expected length of time.

2. Uses of the Future Perfect Continuous Tense

2.1 Ongoing Actions Leading Up to a Future Event:

"By the time you arrive, I will have been working on the project for three hours."

2.2 The Duration of an Activity Until a Future Time:

"They will have been living here for ten years by the end of this month."

2.3 Duration of an Activity in the Future:

"By next year, she will have been studying at the university for four years."

2.4 Highlighting the Cause of a Future Situation:

: "They will be tired because they will have been traveling for twelve hours."

2.5 Expressing Expected or Planned Continuous Activity:

"He will have been living in Paris for a year by the end of this month."

3. Signal Words

By (a specific time)

Example: "By next year, she will have been teaching for a decade."

For (a duration of time)

Example: "For ten years, they will have been living in this house by next December."

When

Example: "When you arrive, I will have been waiting for two hours."

By the time

Example: "By the time he finishes the marathon, he will have been running for over three hours."

Until (a specific time)

Example: "Until midnight, I will have been working on this report for six hours."

4. Structure of the Future Perfect Continuous Tense

Sentence Type Structure Example
Affirmative Subject + will + have been + V1 + -ing + Object Alex Meraj will have been studying for three hours.
Negative Subject + will not (won't) + have been + V1 + -ing + Object Alex Meraj won't have been studying for three hours.
Interrogative Will + Subject + have been + V1 + -ing + Object + ? Will Alex Meraj have been studying for three hours?
Wh-Question Wh-word + will + Subject + have been + V1 + -ing + Object + ? Why will Alex Meraj have been studying for three hours?
Interrogative Negative Won't + Subject + have been + V1 + -ing + Object + ? Won't Alex Meraj have been studying for three hours?

5. Spelling of Verbs in the Future Perfect Continuous Tense

Spelling Rule Base Form Present Participle Example in Future Perfect Continuous
Regular Verbs play playing She will have been playing for two hours by the time you arrive.
Verbs ending in -e make making They will have been making progress on the project for weeks.
One-syllable verbs ending in vowel + consonant (double the final consonant) run running He will have been running for thirty minutes by the time you join him.
Verbs ending in -ie (change -ie to -y) lie lying By next year, they will have been lying about their identity for two years.
Verbs ending in -c (add -k before -ing) picnic picnicking By this afternoon, we will have been picnicking for three hours.
Verbs with final consonant preceded by two vowels (no doubling) read reading She will have been reading that book for a month by then.
Verbs ending in -y (no change) enjoy enjoying He will have been enjoying his vacation for a week by tomorrow.

The sun _______________ (shine) all day by this evening.
