Future Continuous Tense

The future continuous (also known as the future progressive) tense is often used in English as a way to talk about an action or event happening at a given point in the future It indicates that something will occur in the future and continue for an expected length of time.

2. Uses of the Future Continuous Tense

2.1 Actions in Progress in the Future

Describes actions that will be ongoing at a specific future time.

This time tomorow, I will be working on my assignment.

At 8 PM tonight, they will be having dinner.

2.2 Future Plans or Arrangements

Indicates future plans or arrangements, often with a focus on the ongoing nature of the action.

Next month, she will be studying for her exams.

In July, we will be traveling to Europe.

2.3 Polite Inquiries

Used for polite inquiries about someone's plans or intentions for a specific future time.

Will you be attending the conference next week?

Will they be joining us for dinner tonight?

2.4 Future Plans

Highlighting plans that have been made and are expected to take place as scheduled.

She will be giving a presentation at 2 PM on Friday.

We will be celebrating her birthday at the restaurant tomorrow evening."

2.5 Confirmed Activities

Expressing activities that have already been planned and are certain to happen.

He will be teaching the class all day on Tuesday.

They will be moving into their new house next week.

3. Signal Words for Future Continuous Tense

  • At (specific time): "At 9 AM tomorrow, I will be attending a meeting."
  • This time tomorrow/next week/etc.: "This time tomorrow, he will be giving a presentation."
  • In (specific time period): "In two hours, we will be finishing the project."
  • By (specific time): "By noon, they will be arriving at the airport."
  • Tonight/tomorrow/next week/etc.: "Tomorrow evening, we will be celebrating."

4. Table Format: Structure of the Future Continuous Tense

Sentence Type Structure Example
Affirmative Subject + will be + V-ing + Object They will be studying for the exam.
Negative Subject + will not (won't) be + V-ing + Object She won't be attending the party tonight.
Interrogative Will + Subject + be + V-ing + Object Will you be coming to the concert tomorrow?
Wh-Question Wh-word + will + Subject + be + V-ing + Object What will they be doing at 8 PM tonight?
Interrogative Negative Won't + Subject + be + V-ing + Object Won't she be waiting for us at the station?

5. Spelling of Verbs in the Future Continuous Tense

Verb Type Example Base Form Future Continuous Form
Regular Verbs play playing
eat eating
Verbs ending in -e take taking
drive driving
Verbs ending in a single consonant preceded by a single vowel run running
swim swimming
Verbs ending in -ie tie tying
die dying
Irregular Verbs go going
write writing

6. Purpose of Future Continuous Tense

  • Indicate that a more prolonged future activity will be interrupted by a quicker future action.
  • Explain a future action that will occur at a particular moment in time.
  • Talk about a future action that is avoided by another action.

7. Practice Questions of Future Continuous Tense

7.1 Affirmative Sentences:
  • She _______________ (study) English every day.
  • They _______________ (play) basketball on weekends.
  • Alex Meraj _______________ (read) books in his free time.
  • The sun _______________ (rise) in the east.
  • We _______________ (eat) dinner at 7:00 PM.
7.2 Negative Sentences:
  • He will drink coffee every morning.
  • They will watch TV in the evening.
  • She will go to the gym three times a week.
  • Alex Meraj will read the newspaper every day.
  • We will play video games on Fridays.
7.3 Interrogative Sentences:
  • She will speak French fluently.
  • He will work as an engineer.
  • They will live in London.
  • Alex Meraj will study computer science.
  • We will visit our grandparents every Sunday.
7.4 Wh-Question Sentences:
  • _______________ (What) will she be doing in her free time?
  • _______________ (Where) will they be living?
  • _______________ (Why) will he always be arriving late?
  • _______________ (When) will you be going to bed?
  • _______________ (How often) will they be exercising?
7.5 Interrogative Negative Sentences:
  • She will eat meat.
  • They will drink soda.
  • He will play the guitar.
  • Alex Meraj will write poetry.
  • We will watch movies on Fridays.
