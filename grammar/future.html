Simple Future Tense

1. The Simple Future Tense

The Simple Future Tense is used to describe actions or events that will happen in the future. It is used to make predictions, express intentions or plans, and talk about scheduled events or spontaneous decisions. .

2. Uses of the Simple Future

2.1 Predictions:

To make predictions about what will happen in the future.

t will rain tomorrow.

They will win the game.

2.2 Intentions or Plans:

To express a plan or intention for the future.

She will visit her grandparents next week.

I will start a new job in July.

2.3 Spontaneous Decisions:

To indicate decisions made at the moment of speaking.

I will call you back in a minute.

We will go to the movies tonight."

2.4 Scheduled Events:

To refer to events that are scheduled to happen in the future.

The train will depart at 6 PM.

The meeting will start at 9 AM.

2.5 Spontaneous Decisions:

We use 'will' to talk about spontaneous decisions decided at the moment of speaking.

I forgot to call my boyfriend. I'll do it after dinner.

3. Signal Words

These words often indicate the use of the Simple Future Tense:

Tomorrow: "We will leave tomorrow."

Next (week/month/year): "He will start school next year."

Soon: "She will arrive soon."

In (a time period): "I will see you in a week."

Later: "They will call you later."

Tonight: "I will finish the report tonight."

4. Structure of the Simple Future Tense

Sentence Type Structure Example
Affirmative Subject + will + V1 + Object Alex Meraj will become a millionaire.
Negative Subject + will not (won't) + V1 + Object Alex Meraj won't own a billion-dollar company.
Interrogative Will + Subject + V1 + Object + ? Will Alex Meraj aspire to be a billionaire?
Wh-Question Wh-word + will + Subject + V1 + Object + ? What will Alex Meraj do for a living?
Interrogative Negative Won't + Subject + V1 + Object + ? Won't Alex Meraj donate to charities?

5. Spelling of Verbs in the Simple Future

Since the Simple Future Tense uses the auxiliary verb "will" followed by the base form of the main verb (V1), there are no specific spelling changes to the verbs themselves. The form remains consistent:

Spelling Rule Example Third Person Singular
Regular Verbs: play will play
eat will eat
write will write
Verbs ending in -s, -ss, -x, -ch, -sh: kiss will kiss
fix will fix
watch will watch
Verbs ending in consonant + y: carry will carry
hurry will hurry
worry will worry
Verbs ending in vowel + y: play will play
say will say
Verbs ending in -o: go will go
do will do

6. How to Use Special Verbs in the Simple Future

Special Verb Pronouns Affirmative Sentence Negative Sentence
Will I I will go to the park. I will not (won't) go to the park.
You You will learn Spanish. You will not (won't) learn Spanish.
He/She/It He will finish his homework. He will not (won't) finish his homework.
We We will meet them tomorrow. We will not (won't) meet them tomorrow.
They They will travel to Japan. They will not (won't) travel to Japan.
Shall I I shall go to the meeting. I shall not (shan't) go to the meeting.
We We shall overcome the obstacles. We shall not (shan't) overcome the obstacles.
Can I I can help you tomorrow. I cannot (can't) help you tomorrow.
You You can achieve your goals. You cannot (can't) achieve your goals.
He/She/It She can join us later. She cannot (can't) join us later.
We We can finish this project. We cannot (can't) finish this project.
They They can come to the party. They cannot (can't) come to the party.
May I I may attend the conference. I may not attend the conference.
You You may leave early. You may not leave early.

We We may go on vacation. We may not go on vacation.

7. Practice Questions of Simple Future Tense

7.1 Affirmative Sentences:

Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verb in the Simple Future Tense:

  • She _______________ (study) English every day.
  • They _______________ (play) basketball on weekends.
  • Alex Meraj _______________ (read) books in his free time.
  • The sun _______________ (rise) in the east.
  • We _______________ (eat) dinner at 7:00 PM.
7.2 Negative Sentences:

Rewrite the following sentences in the negative form using the Simple Future Tense:

  • He will drink coffee every morning.
  • They will watch TV in the evening.
  • She will go to the gym three times a week.
  • Alex Meraj will read the newspaper every day.
  • We will play video games on Fridays.
7.3 Interrogative Sentences:

Ask questions using the Simple Future Tense for the following statements:

  • She will speak French fluently.
  • He will work as an engineer.
  • They will live in London.
  • Alex Meraj will study computer science.
  • We will visit our grandparents every Sunday.
7.4 Wh-Question Sentences:

Ask questions beginning with the appropriate question word:

  • _______________ ( ) will she do in her free time?
  • _______________ ( ) will they live?
  • _______________ ( ) will he always arrive late?
  • _______________ ( ) will you go to bed?
  • _______________ ( ) often will they exercise?
7.5 Interrogative Negative Sentences:

Rewrite the following sentences in the interrogative negative form using the Simple Future Tense:

  • She will eat meat.
  • They will drink soda.
  • He will play the guitar.
  • Alex Meraj will write poetry.
  • We will watch movies on Fridays.

  • We will eat dinner at 7:00 PM.
