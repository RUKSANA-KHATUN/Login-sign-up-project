Present Perfect Tense

The Present Perfect tense is a grammatical construction that indicates a connection between the past and the present. It is formed by using the auxiliary verb "have" or "has" followed by the past participle of the main verb.

This tense is particularly unique because it expresses actions or events that occurred at an indefinite time in the past, yet have relevance or significance to the present moment.

1. Use of the Present Perfect
1.1 Recently completed actions

He has just played football.

1.2 Actions or events that have occurred repeatedly up to the present moment

We have watched that movie several times.

1.3 Experiences or accomplishments in one's life

He has traveled to many countries.

1.4 Together with lately, recently, yet

I have been to London recently.

1.5 Actions or events that started in the past and continue into the present

She has lived in this city for five years.

2. Signal words

Already

Suggests that an action or event has occurred before the present moment.

She has already finished her homework.

Since

Specifies a point in time in the past from which an action or event started and continues into the present.

I have known her since high school.

Just

Indicates that an action or event has recently occurred.

She has just arrived home.

Lately

Refers to actions or events that have been happening frequently in the recent past.

He has been feeling tired lately.

So far

Indicates the extent of an action or event up to the present moment.

We have had a great vacation so far.


3. Structure of the Present Perfect Tense
Sentence Type Structure Example
Affirmative Subject + have/has + past participle + Object She has studied English.
Negative Subject + have/has + not + past participle + Object They haven't finished yet.
Interrogative Have/Has + Subject + past participle + Object + ? Have you seen the movie?
Interrogative-Negative Have/Has + Subject + not + past participle + Object + ? Hasn't she eaten dinner yet?

4. How to Use Special Verbs in the Present Perfect Tense
Special Verb Pronouns Affirmative Sentence Negative Sentence
Can I/You/He/She/It/We/They I can have finished my homework.
She can have known the answer.		 I can't have forgotten my keys.
They can't have left already.
May I/You/He/She/It/We/They He may have lost the document.
They may have arrived late.		 She may not have seen the message.

Might I/You/He/She/It/We/They You might have heard the news.

You must not have been there.

They should have arrived by now.		 I shouldn't have said that.

5. How to Spell Regular and Irregular Verbs in the Present Perfect Tense
Spelling Rule Example
Adding -ed
Double the consonant after short, stressed vowel stop → I have stopped.
swap → She has swapped her books.
Only add -d after one -e at the end of the word love → He has loved her for years.
save → They have saved money.
Change -y to -i after a consonant, then add -ed worry → She has worried about the outcome.
Add -ed with verbs ending in -y preceded by a vowel (a, e, i, o, u) play → They have played football.
Irregular Verbs
Use the 3rd column of the irregular verb list		 (go → went → gone)

Practice Questions of Present Perfect Tense
6 Affirmative Sentences

Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verb in parentheses in the Present Perfect Tense:

  • She ____________ (visit) her grandparents several times this year.
  • They ____________ (complete) the project successfully.
  • Alex Meraj ____________ (read) the book you recommended.
  • The sun ____________ (rise) already.
  • We ____________ (eat) dinner together every weekend.
6.1 Negative Sentences

Rewrite the following sentences in the negative form using the Present Perfect Tense:

  • He ____________ (drink) coffee every morning.
  • They ____________ (watch) the new TV series.
  • She ____________ (go) to the gym this week.
  • Alex Meraj ____________ (write) the report.
  • We ____________ (play) video games recently.
6.2 Interrogative Sentences

Form questions using the Present Perfect Tense for the following statements:

  • ____________ (she/speak) French fluently?
  • ____________ (he/work) as an engineer?
  • ____________ (they/live) in London?
  • ____________ (Alex Meraj/study) computer science?
  • ____________ (we/visit) our grandparents this month?
6.3 Wh-Question Sentences

Form questions beginning with the appropriate question word:

  • ____________ (What) ____________ (you/do) in your free time?
  • ____________ (Where) ____________ (they/live) for the past year?
  • ____________ (Why) ____________ (he/arrive) late?
  • ____________ (When) ____________ (you/go) to bed?
  • ____________ (How long) ____________ (they/exercise) regularly?
6.4 Interrogative Negative Sentences

Rewrite the following sentences in the interrogative negative form using the Present Perfect Tense:

  • ____________ (she/eat) meat?
  • ____________ (they/drink) soda?
  • ____________ (he/play) the guitar?
  • ____________ (Alex Meraj/write) poetry?
  • ____________ (we/watch) movies recently?
Answers:

6 Affirmative Sentences:

  • She has visited her grandparents several times this year.
  • They have completed the project successfully.
  • Alex Meraj has read the book you recommended.
  • The sun has risen already.
  • We have eaten dinner together every weekend.

6.1 Negative Sentences:

  • He has not (hasn't) drunk coffee every morning.
  • They have not (haven't) watched the new TV series.
  • She has not (hasn't) gone to the gym this week.
  • Alex Meraj has not (hasn't) written the report.
  • We have not (haven't) played video games recently.

6.2 Interrogative Sentences:

  • Has she spoken French fluently?
  • Has he worked as an engineer?
  • Have they lived in London?
  • Has Alex Meraj studied computer science?
  • Have we visited our grandparents this month?

6.3 Wh-Question Sentences:

  • What have you done in your free time?
  • Where have they lived for the past year?
  • Why has he arrived late?
  • When have you gone to bed?
  • How long have they exercised regularly?

6.4 Interrogative Negative Sentences:

  • Hasn't she eaten meat?
  • Haven't they drunk soda?
  • Hasn't he played the guitar?
  • Hasn't Alex Meraj written poetry?
  • Haven't we watched movies recently?
