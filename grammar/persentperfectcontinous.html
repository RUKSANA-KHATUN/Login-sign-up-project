What is the Present Perfect Continuous Tense?

The present perfect continuous tense (also known as the present perfect progressive) just like the present perfect simple can be used to talk about past actions or states which are still connected to the present.

1. Use of the Present Perfect Progressive

1.1 Recent or current activities

I have been reading this book.

1.2 Temporary actions or situations

He has been living with his parents while he looks for a job.

1.3 Expressions of irritation or annoyance about repeated actions

She has been talking on the phone all morning, and it's driving me crazy!

1.4 Actions that started in the past and are still ongoing

She has been working at the company for five years.

2. Signal words

How long

Used in questions to inquire about the duration of the ongoing action

How long have you been studying?

Continuously

Emphasizes that the action has been happening without interruption.

The machine has been running continuously for hours.

Since + specific time

Specifies a particular time when the action began.

I have been living here since 2010.

The whole day

Is another signal phrase indicating the duration of an action or event throughout the entire day.

They have been working on the project the whole day.

For

Indicates the duration of time that the action has been happening.

She has been studying English for three hours.

3. Structure of the Present Perfect Continuous Tense

Sentence Type Structure Example
Affirmative Subject + have/has + been + present participle (verb + -ing) + Object They have been studying English.
Negative Subject + have/has + not + been + present participle (verb + -ing) + Object She hasn't been working here for long.
Interrogative Have/Has + Subject + been + present participle (verb + -ing) + Object + ? Have you been waiting for a long time?
Interrogative-Negative Have/Has + Subject + not + been + present participle (verb + -ing) + Object + ? Hasn't she been sleeping well lately?

4. Spelling of verbs in the Present Perfect Progressive

Verb Type Example Present Perfect Progressive Form
Regular Verbs play have been playing
walk have been walking
Verbs ending in -e write have been writing
dance have been dancing
Verbs ending in a consonant + vowel + consonant stop have been stopping
swim have been swimming
Verbs ending in -ie lie have been lying
die have been dying
Verbs ending in -y (preceded by a consonant) carry have been carrying
study have been studying

4. Spelling of verbs in the Present Perfect Continuous Tense

Spelling Rule Example
Regular Verbs (Base Form (Infinitive) + -ing) They have been studying English.
She hasn't been working here for long.
Have you been waiting for a long time?
Hasn't she been sleeping well lately?
Irregular Verbs: Varied past participle forms (refer to irregular verb list) They have been swimming in the pool.
He hasn't been feeling well recently.
Have they been speaking to the manager?
Hasn't he been running for an hour now?

5. Tenses Comparison

Tense Structure Uses Example (Affirmative) Example (Negative) Example (Interrogative)
Simple Present Subject + V1 (base form) / Subject + V1-s/es (3rd person singular) Habitual actions, general truths, repeated actions, or fixed arrangements She studies English every day. She does not (doesn't) study English. Does she study English every day?
Present Perfect Subject + has/have + V3 (past participle) Action that happened at an unspecified time in the past, or that started in the past and continues to present She has studied English. She has not (hasn't) studied English. Has she studied English?
Present Continuous Subject + am/is/are + V-ing Action happening right now, or a temporary action She is studying English. She is not (isn't) studying English. Is she studying English?
Present Perfect Continuous Subject + has/have + been + V-ing Action that started in the past and is still continuing, or was recently completed with focus on duration She has been studying English. She has not (hasn't) been studying English. Has she been studying English?

6. Questions in Present Perfect Continuous Tense

6.1 Affirmative Sentences

Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verb in the Present Perfect Continuous tense:

She _______________ (study) English for two hours.

They _______________ (play) basketball since morning.

Alex Meraj _______________ (read) books in his free time.

The sun _______________ (shine) since we arrived.

We _______________ (eat) dinner together every night.

6.2 Negative Sentences

Rewrite the following sentences in the negative form using the Present Perfect Continuous tense:

He has been drinking coffee every morning.
He _______________ _______________ coffee every morning.

They have been watching TV in the evening.
They _______________ _______________ TV in the evening.

She has been going to the gym three times a week.
She _______________ _______________ to the gym three times a week.

Alex Meraj has been reading the newspaper every day.
Alex Meraj _______________ _______________ the newspaper every day.

We have been playing video games on Fridays.
We _______________ _______________ video games on Fridays.

6.3 Interrogative Sentences

Ask questions using the Present Perfect Continuous tense for the following statements:

She has been speaking French fluently.
_______________ she _______________ French fluently?

_______________ he _______________ as an engineer?

They have been living in London.
_______________ they _______________ in London?

Alex Meraj has been studying computer science.
_______________ Alex Meraj _______________ computer science?

We have been visiting our grandparents every Sunday.
_______________ we _______________ our grandparents every Sunday?

6.4 Wh-Question Sentences

Ask questions beginning with the appropriate question word:
