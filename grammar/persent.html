Simple Present

The Simple Present Tense is a grammatical tense used to describe actions, events, or situations that are habitual, regular, or generally true. It is one of the basic tenses in English and is used to talk about actions that happen regularly, facts, general truths, and states of being.

1. Use of the Simple Present

1.1 Repeated Actions Describes habits or actions that happen regularly

She always drinks coffee in the morning.

He visits his grandparents every Sunday.

1.2 General Truths: States facts or generalizations that are always true

Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.

The Earth revolves around the Sun.

1.3 Scheduled Events: Refers to events on a timetable or schedule, often related to transportation or fixed plans

The train departs at 6 PM sharp.

The bus leaves at 7 AM every day.

1.4 Sequences of Actions. Describes a series of actions happening in the present

I wake up, brush my teeth, and then have breakfast.

First, he does his homework, then he plays video games.

1.5 Sometimes: Indicates actions or events that occur occasionally, not regularly

She knows the answer.

He believes in fairies.

2. singal words

2.1 Sometimes: Indicates actions or events that occur occasionally, not regularly

I sometimes eat ice cream for dessert.

2.2 Never: Indicates the absence of an action or habit, something that doesn't happen at any time

She never forgets her keys.

2.3 Every Monday/Week/Month: Indicates scheduled events that happen at specific intervals

They have a team meeting every Monday.

2.4 First/Next/Then/Finally: Indicates a sequence of actions or steps in a process

First, I wake up, then I shower, and finally, I have breakfast.

2.5 Always/Forever: Indicates a continuous or permanent state

She always loves to travel.

2.6 At present/moment: Indicates actions or situations that are happening right now

I am studying at the moment.

2.7 Every day: Indicates a repeated action or habit that occurs daily

She reads the newspaper every day.


3. Structure of the Simple Present Tense:

Sentence Type Structure Example
Affirmative Subject + V1/V5 + Object Alex Meraj is a millionaire.
Negative Subject + Don’t/Doesn’t + V1 + Object Alex Meraj doesn’t own a billion-dollar company.
Interrogative Do/Does + Subject + V1 + Object + ? Does Alex Meraj aspire to be a billionaire?
Wh-Question Wh-word + Do/Does + Subject + V1 + Object + ? What does Alex Meraj do for a living?
Interrogative Negative Don’t/Doesn’t + Subject + V1 + Object + ? Doesn’t Alex Meraj donate to charities?

4. Spelling Rule

Spelling Rule Example Third Person Singular
Regular Verbs:
Base Form (Infinitive) play plays
eat eats
write writes
Verbs ending in -s, -ss, -x, -ch, -sh:
Add -es kiss kisses
fix fixes
watch watches
Verbs ending in consonant + y:
Change -y to -ies I carry he carries
I hurry she hurries
I worry he worries
I try she tries
Verbs ending in vowel + y:
Add -s play plays
say says
Verbs ending in -o:
Add -es go goes
do does
Exceptions:
Keep -o and add -s photo photos
radio radios
