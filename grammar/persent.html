Simple Present

The Simple Present Tense is a grammatical tense used to describe actions, events, or situations that are habitual, regular, or generally true. It is one of the basic tenses in English and is used to talk about actions that happen regularly, facts, general truths, and states of being.

1. Use of the Simple Present

1.1 Repeated Actions Describes habits or actions that happen regularly

She always drinks coffee in the morning.

He visits his grandparents every Sunday.

1.2 General Truths: States facts or generalizations that are always true.

"Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
